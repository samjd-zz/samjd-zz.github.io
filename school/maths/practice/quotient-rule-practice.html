üìê Quotient Rule Practice Problems

Quotient Rule Formula:
$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right] = \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2}$$ When: Derivative of one function divided by another

üåü Basic Level

1 Find the derivative of $$f(x) = \frac{x^2}{x+1}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = x^2\) and \(g(x) = x+1\)
Then \(f'(x) = 2x\) and \(g'(x) = 1\)

$$f'(x) = \frac{2x(x+1) - x^2(1)}{(x+1)^2} = \frac{2x^2 + 2x - x^2}{(x+1)^2} = \frac{x^2 + 2x}{(x+1)^2}$$
2 Find the derivative of $$y = \frac{2x+3}{x-1}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = 2x+3\) and \(g(x) = x-1\)
Then \(f'(x) = 2\) and \(g'(x) = 1\)

$$y' = \frac{2(x-1) - (2x+3)(1)}{(x-1)^2} = \frac{2x - 2 - 2x - 3}{(x-1)^2} = \frac{-5}{(x-1)^2}$$
3 Find the derivative of $$h(x) = \frac{x^3}{x^2+1}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = x^3\) and \(g(x) = x^2+1\)
Then \(f'(x) = 3x^2\) and \(g'(x) = 2x\)

$$h'(x) = \frac{3x^2(x^2+1) - x^3(2x)}{(x^2+1)^2} = \frac{3x^4 + 3x^2 - 2x^4}{(x^2+1)^2} = \frac{x^4 + 3x^2}{(x^2+1)^2}$$
4 Find the derivative of $$g(x) = \frac{5x}{x^2+4}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = 5x\) and \(g(x) = x^2+4\)
Then \(f'(x) = 5\) and \(g'(x) = 2x\)

$$g'(x) = \frac{5(x^2+4) - 5x(2x)}{(x^2+4)^2} = \frac{5x^2 + 20 - 10x^2}{(x^2+4)^2} = \frac{20 - 5x^2}{(x^2+4)^2}$$

‚ö° Intermediate Level

5 Find the derivative of $$f(x) = \frac{x^2-3x+2}{x^2+2x}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = x^2-3x+2\) and \(g(x) = x^2+2x\)
Then \(f'(x) = 2x-3\) and \(g'(x) = 2x+2\)

Apply the quotient rule:
$$f'(x) = \frac{(2x-3)(x^2+2x) - (x^2-3x+2)(2x+2)}{(x^2+2x)^2}$$ Expand \((2x-3)(x^2+2x)\):
$$= 2x^3 + 4x^2 - 3x^2 - 6x = 2x^3 + x^2 - 6x$$ Expand \((x^2-3x+2)(2x+2)\):
$$= 2x^3 + 2x^2 - 6x^2 - 6x + 4x + 4 = 2x^3 - 4x^2 - 2x + 4$$ Subtract:
$$= \frac{(2x^3 + x^2 - 6x) - (2x^3 - 4x^2 - 2x + 4)}{(x^2+2x)^2}$$ $$= \frac{2x^3 + x^2 - 6x - 2x^3 + 4x^2 + 2x - 4}{(x^2+2x)^2}$$ $$= \frac{5x^2 - 4x - 4}{(x^2+2x)^2}$$
6 Find the derivative of $$y = \frac{\sin x}{x}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = \sin x\) and \(g(x) = x\)
Then \(f'(x) = \cos x\) and \(g'(x) = 1\)

$$y' = \frac{\cos x \cdot x - \sin x \cdot 1}{x^2} = \frac{x\cos x - \sin x}{x^2}$$
7 Find the derivative of $$p(x) = \frac{e^x}{x^2+1}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = e^x\) and \(g(x) = x^2+1\)
Then \(f'(x) = e^x\) and \(g'(x) = 2x\)

$$p'(x) = \frac{e^x(x^2+1) - e^x(2x)}{(x^2+1)^2} = \frac{e^x(x^2+1-2x)}{(x^2+1)^2} = \frac{e^x(x^2-2x+1)}{(x^2+1)^2}$$
8 Find the derivative of $$q(x) = \frac{x^2+1}{e^x}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = x^2+1\) and \(g(x) = e^x\)
Then \(f'(x) = 2x\) and \(g'(x) = e^x\)

$$q'(x) = \frac{2x \cdot e^x - (x^2+1) \cdot e^x}{(e^x)^2} = \frac{e^x(2x - x^2 - 1)}{e^{2x}} = \frac{2x - x^2 - 1}{e^x}$$
9 Find the derivative of $$r(x) = \frac{\ln x}{x^2}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = \ln x\) and \(g(x) = x^2\)
Then \(f'(x) = \frac{1}{x}\) and \(g'(x) = 2x\)

$$r'(x) = \frac{\frac{1}{x} \cdot x^2 - \ln x \cdot 2x}{x^4} = \frac{x - 2x\ln x}{x^4} = \frac{1 - 2\ln x}{x^3}$$

üöÄ Advanced Level

10 Find the derivative of $$f(x) = \frac{x\cos x}{1+\sin x}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = x\cos x\) and \(g(x) = 1+\sin x\)
Using product rule: \(f'(x) = \cos x - x\sin x\)
And \(g'(x) = \cos x\)

$$f'(x) = \frac{(\cos x - x\sin x)(1+\sin x) - x\cos x \cdot \cos x}{(1+\sin x)^2}$$ $$= \frac{\cos x + \cos x\sin x - x\sin x - x\sin^2 x - x\cos^2 x}{(1+\sin x)^2}$$ $$= \frac{\cos x + \cos x\sin x - x\sin x - x}{(1+\sin x)^2}$$
11 Find the derivative of $$g(x) = \frac{x^3-2x}{(x+1)^2}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = x^3-2x\) and \(g(x) = (x+1)^2 = x^2+2x+1\)
Then \(f'(x) = 3x^2-2\) and \(g'(x) = 2x+2 = 2(x+1)\)

$$g'(x) = \frac{(3x^2-2)(x+1)^2 - (x^3-2x) \cdot 2(x+1)}{(x+1)^4}$$ $$= \frac{(x+1)[(3x^2-2)(x+1) - 2(x^3-2x)]}{(x+1)^4}$$ $$= \frac{3x^3 + 3x^2 - 2x - 2 - 2x^3 + 4x}{(x+1)^3} = \frac{x^3 + 3x^2 + 2x - 2}{(x+1)^3}$$
12 Find the derivative of $$h(x) = \frac{\tan x}{1+x^2}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = \tan x\) and \(g(x) = 1+x^2\)
Then \(f'(x) = \sec^2 x\) and \(g'(x) = 2x\)

$$h'(x) = \frac{\sec^2 x(1+x^2) - \tan x \cdot 2x}{(1+x^2)^2} = \frac{\sec^2 x(1+x^2) - 2x\tan x}{(1+x^2)^2}$$
13 Find the derivative of $$k(x) = \frac{e^{2x}}{x^2+x+1}$$
Solution:
Let \(f(x) = e^{2x}\) and \(g(x) = x^2+x+1\)
Then \(f'(x) = 2e^{2x}\) and \(g'(x) = 2x+1\)

$$k'(x) = \frac{2e^{2x}(x^2+x+1) - e^{2x}(2x+1)}{(x^2+x+1)^2}$$ $$= \frac{e^{2x}[2(x^2+x+1) - (2x+1)]}{(x^2+x+1)^2}$$ $$= \frac{e^{2x}(2x^2+2x+2-2x-1)}{(x^2+x+1)^2} = \frac{e^{2x}(2x^2+1)}{(x^2+x+1)^2}$$

üí° Tips for Success:

  • Always identify f(x) (numerator) and g(x) (denominator) first
  • Find f'(x) and g'(x) separately before applying the formula
  • Don't forget to square the denominator: [g(x)]¬≤
  • Watch your signs! The minus sign in the numerator is crucial
  • Simplify your final answer when possible
  • For complex functions, use the chain rule or product rule as needed within f'(x) or g'(x)
